Performance




Performance of the model shows sign of overfitting, but the issue is not too bad, testing still show relatively high performance of the model, in terms of log-loss, f1 and precision value. ROC curve shows that the model could vary quite significantly in result, in this case of 9%. Back to the result given however, it is shown that a IR system's performance is highly correlated to the use of ranking, bm (best matching), weights and e-ranking, all which suggests that IR system that uses ranking method one way of another would normally obtain better performance. Which would make sense considering that IR system and the current TREC-COVID challenge and research mainly uses ranking based evaluating metrics for evaluation of the performance of the IR. Either way, IR system that considers ranking seems to have quite a strong correlation with the outcome of their performance, based on the model that we obtained.





About the project


The evaluator that is shown is a classifier model that is trained on TREC-COVID's results from each participants. TREC-COVI is an organised event that would liketo further the research on deployment of Information Retrieval (IR) system. This is done by organising competition (in Kaggle) or through volunteers where each participant then tries to provide the best IR system for solving certain question related to COVID, providing relevant information for the medical experts researching on the COVID matters. Our evaluator is trying to figure out whether a certain parameters, or use of different methods or tools would affect the result of the participant's IR system in any manner.
